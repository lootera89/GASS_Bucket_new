Future of online gaming in india

Posted on March 30, 2023 by Tushar garg aggrawal

In recent years, the online gaming industry in India has witnessed an unprecedented boom, driven by factors such as increasing internet penetration and availability of affordable smartphones. With millions of gamers logging on to platforms like PUBG Mobile and Call of Duty every day, it is clear that online gaming has emerged as a mainstream form of entertainment for Indian audiences. But what lies ahead for this rapidly-evolving sector? In this blog post, we explore the future of online gaming in India - from emerging trends and challenges to potential growth opportunities - to help you stay updated with one of the most exciting industries in the country.

current state of online gaming in India

The current state of online gaming in India is fascinating. The country has the second largest population of gamers in the world, with an estimated 50 million people playing online games. This number is expected to grow to 500 million by 2025. The majority of Indian gamers are between the ages of 18 and 35, and most play on their smartphones.

The most popular genres of games in India are multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA), first-person shooter (FPS), and real-time strategy (RTS). Popular games include PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (PUBG), Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (Banned due to Chinese origin), Gareena Free Fire and Call of Duty: Mobile. In 2023,  PUBG alone had 200 million active players in India.

The online gaming industry in India is currently worth $890 million and is expected to reach $5 billion by 2025. The majority of this revenue comes from mobile gamers, who account for 65% of the market. In-game purchases are also a significant source of revenue, accounting for 30% of the market. The remaining 5% comes from advertising and sponsorships.

The Indian government has been supportive of the growth of the online gaming industry. In 2019, they launched a dedicated committee to promote esports in the country. In 2023 They have also agreed to esports being an actual serious sports. These initiatives will help ensure that the online gaming industry continues to thrive in India.

The potential for online gaming in India

The potential for online gaming in India is vast. With a population of over 1.3 billion people and a growing middle class, the market for online games is huge. In addition, India has a young population with a median age of 27 years old, which is prime age for gaming. Moreover, internet usage is growing rapidly in India with the number of internet users expected to reach 1.5 billion by 2024. This presents a major opportunity for game developers and publishers to tap into the Indian market.

There are already numerous online games available in India, ranging from simple casual games to more complex multiplayer games. However, there is still room for growth in the market. For example, many Indians still prefer to play offline games due to concerns about internet connectivity and data charges. In addition, piracy is also a major issue in India. This means that there is an opportunity for game developers and publishers to develop anti-piracy measures specifically for the Indian market.

overall, the potential for online gaming in India is immense. With a large and growing population, a young demographic, and increasing internet penetration, the market presents a major opportunity for growth.

The challenges facing online gaming in India

The online gaming industry in India is growing at a rapid pace, but there are still some challenges that need to be addressed in order to make it truly successful. One of the biggest challenges is the low broadband penetration in the country. According to a report by The Internet and Mobile Association of India, only 10 percent of households in India have a broadband connection. This means that a large number of potential gamers are not able to enjoy online games due to the slow internet speeds.

Another challenge facing online gaming in India is the lack of awareness about the benefits of playing games online. Many people still believe that gaming is a waste of time and money. This needs to change if the industry is going to grow significantly. Gaming companies need to educate people about the benefits of playing online games, such as improving problem-solving skills and hand-eye coordination.

Finally, another challenge facing online gaming in India is the high cost of mobile data. According to a report by Deloitte, the average cost of 1GB of mobile data in India is $0.27, which is one of the highest in the world. This makes it difficult for people to afford data plans that would allow them to play online games regularly.
